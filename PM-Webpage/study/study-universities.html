WHAT IS ENTRANCE EXAM? 


Finland is very famous for its good and advanced education. For that reason, in recent years, a lot of foreign students tend to attend University here.

Applying for University in Finland is not too difficult, because they are unlikely to evaluate through your high school result. You can apply in three main ways: using the result of IELTS, using the result of the SAT, and using the result of the Entrance exam. While IELTS and SAT are familiar to almost all students, the Entrance exam is still not too clear. They still confuse about the Entrance exam and wonder if it is easier and more money-consuming than taking the SAT and IELTS Test. 

Reading this article, you will have a whole picture of this way to apply for University - Entrance exam - to have a final suitable decision.

 

 

1) What is an Entrance exam?

 

- In Finland, entrance examinations are used frequently as part of student admission to apply for Bachelor’s level education. Each major and program have different criteria and method sections. For that reason, you should check which are the suitable ones and you are interested in the study program description. If the study programs that you apply for are included in one major, you just need to complete one exam, and so on (follow the table below). Entrance examinations can take place in 2-3 phases. Only when you pass the previous phase, can you continue with the next test in the Entrance exam?

- The application annually takes place from 7 January - 12 February. The Entrance exam will be held from March to May, depending on the study program that you apply for.
