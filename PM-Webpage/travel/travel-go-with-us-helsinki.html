GO WITH US 1 - HELSINKI

 

Have you ever questioned, “Why Finland becomes the happiest country in the world”? Go to Finland with our series “GO WITH US”, you will soon get the answer.

I’m pretty sure that many people think that Finland is very small and there are not many tourist attractions, so they hardly choose Finland as a must-visit destination when traveling. But they will soon change that thought when first visiting Finland, especially their capital: Helsinki. You will be surprised by the many impressive things here.

I’ll open the series “GO WITH US” in Helsinki, and let’s go explore this place with us!

 

 

1) Why Finland and Helsinki are not as boring as people think? 

 

  1.  Helsinki is globally considered as the design center of the world in 2012

    Finland has a long history of art and design, but it is not too famous because Finns are very modest with their achievement. But if you are an art lover, Helsinki will attract you for the very first time with their unique and significant product in every corner of this city.

    When visiting this city, you can easily find many arty structures and architecture. It seems like you are in an actual designing world.

    For that reason, Helsinki is not inferior to any city in Europe in that you must pay the visit once. 

  2. Experience peace and silenceAlthough Helsinki is the capital of a country, it has its own pace in daily life, which makes the visitors feel very comfortable and relaxed. Surrounded by many large forests and countless lakes, travelers visiting this place will have a calm and sought-for relaxing time. There are many activities that are close to nature, which give you a memorable and peaceful time, different from the active capital like in other countries.

 

 

 

2) What is the ideal time to visit Helsinki?

 

Summer is the most highly recommended time to visit Helsinki. As the second-northmost country capital in the world, the daylight from May to August can extend to 15 hours. For that, traveling at this time is very ideal that you have more time to travel as well as with those who really like photographing due to its beautiful long daylight.

Unless you plan to visit Lapland, you shouldn’t travel to this country in Winter because it will be extremely cold. The temperature in Helsinki can decrease to -7 Celsius Degree this time, so you are not able to visit most of the places and join in outdoor activities as well. 

 

3) How can we arrive in Helsinki?

 

 

Helsinki is the capital of Finland, so don’t worry about transportation. There are about 350 flights per day to Helsinki airport - the largest airport in this country. Also, Helsinki airport service includes all the flights from Europe, Asia, America, so you have plentiful options for your suitable airport brand and time. 

Besides, from Helsinki, it is very easy for you to get access to other cities and areas in Finland. You can go by private car, public transport: bus, train, subway, etc. 

To sum up, starting your trip from Helsinki is reasonable.

 

4) Where to go in Helsinki?

 

 

a. Suomenlinna Island

Suomenlinna is famous for its historic valuations with the remnants of battle fortresses and bunkers. This island is the European model of army architecture in the war period. Suomenlinna was considered a World Cultural Heritage by UNESCO in 1991. 

When visiting here, you can enjoy King Gate, The Great Courtyard, Augustin Ehrensvard, etc. This Island has become the national place that all visitors should pay a visit once, especially with those who are fond of European history and nature.

b. Esplanade Park

This park is directly located in the center, so it is also the place where to hold many holidays and activities in Helsinki.

The park has plenty of trees with some statues of historic heroes of Finland. Therefore, this park is an ideal place to take a walk and relax with your partners.

c. Helsinki Cathedral

This Lutheran Church is the symbol of this city that travelers shouldn’t miss when in Helsinki. If you are lucky to visit Finland on some official meaningful holiday, the Senate Square lying below the Cathedral is where you can join in many events.

d. National Museum

The National Museum is a combination of cathedral and palace to introduce Finland’s history. If you are interested in this country’s historic events, you should spare half of the day to enjoy this place.

e. Oodi National Library

Finns are famous for reading books that UNESCO recognized Finland as the highest reading rate country in 2016. Their government also believe that library is symbolized for the importance of education, so they are willing to spend lots of money to build the libraries around Finland. 

Oodi National Library was voted the best public library in the world with around 2 million visitors per year. Don’t pass this helpful place.

 

If you have once visited Finland, do not miss the capital - Helsinki. It is an amazing place where you can find both the active side and the silent side of a city. If you have planned all for your trip to Finland, it’s time to find more about the local food here at “ “EAT” HELSINKI” or explore more cities and places to visit “GO WITH US 2 - LAPLAND”
