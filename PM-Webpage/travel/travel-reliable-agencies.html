ECONOMICAL TRAVELING TO FINLAND

 

Most European countries are developed countries, so traveling these countries is not cheap and so is Finland. With the country covered by many lakes and forests, Finland is the place that you shouldn’t miss when in Europe.

But in fact, due to the high traveling cost, Finland also offers many deal for visitors as well as some tips that you can travel on your budget.

Here are some tips that you can notice when planning to travel to Santa Claus' hometown country. I believe it will support your finances a lot but still have a great trip!

 

 

1) Eat at the buffets 

 While in other countries, buffet seems like a luxury meal, in Finland, you only need to pay around €12 ($13.50 USD) to have a great meal in various restaurants. Also, take the buffet, you can enjoy many local cuisines at one time. Therefore, think of buffet when traveling here as well as take the advantages from this, you will get the pleasure.

  

2) Drink the tap water

 Don’t hesitate to use the tap water at every public area. It not only saves your money from buying the new bottles whenever you need, but also is very friendly to the environment that reduces the amount of PET bottles. Don’t be afraid that the water is truly clean and safe to use. 

 

3) Couchsurf

 Couchsurfing is not unfamiliar anymore with traveling lovers. Accomodation is the most concerning issue because of its price, especially when you have a long trip. By using couchsurfing, you can not only save a huge amount of money, but also have opportunities to experience the local life. With the country of high cost living, couchsurfing is the most economical option for you. All you need to do is buy a beer for the host to thank for their support. 

You can find your couchsurfing at: www.couchsurfing.com for all the trip around the world.

 

4) Grocery shop

 Grocery is an amazing place where you can find all the necessary stuff: bread, meat, chese, fruit or quick meals as well. The price is cheap and can save you a bit of cash. Moreover, grocery shop offers you with plentiful local products, so you can support their economy as well as get access to traditional life. 

 

5)Take the free city tours 

One of the advantages when travelling in European countries is that they provide you with free walking tours in various cities. It is exciting that you don’t have to pay any amount of money, but also have a nice trip to see the city attractions, historic places and enjoy the fresh atmosphere. 

This tour can easily be found on the website (www.freetours.com) or you can freely ask the receptionist or the host where you live

 

6) Get the Helsinki Card 

It is worth getting a Helsinki card when traveling in Helsinki, especially if you have intention to have a sightseeing around the city. The card includes free public transportation, free ticket to some top tourist attraction, and free sightseeing tours by bus or boat. 

You have 3 options for your card to choose the suitable one:

  • 1-day card: €44
  • 2-day card: €52
  • 3-day card: €59

You can add more options to your card by paying extra amount, such as: public transit, regional transit.

 

7) Get a HSL card

HSL card are available on all public transportation of Finland. You need to pay €5 for a new card, Using this card, you can buy either season tickets or pay directly for your trip with the amount of money that you have put into the card. It is highly recommend to load your card if you are not tending to stay in Finland for so long. This is also the cheapest and most convenient way to get around this country.


8) Save money on rideshares 

Using rideshare,  you can share your ride with other passengers to save money. Especially, suitable for those who don’t like to wait for the public transportation and taxi.

You can also find a rideshare around the country through various apps: Uberpool,Kyydit, Carpool World.

These apps will connect you with the drivers and extra passengers. The price is a lot cheaper.

 

9) Don’t travel alone

 If you can travel with a group of people, the price can be reduced a lot. You can share the accommodation, the transportation, the meals and so on. Also, travelling with friends or family may support you in culture shock and facing with many troubles during the trip. Therefore, don’t try travelling by themselves.

 

If you still have confusion of traveling to Finland, go ahead reading our articles to explore how wonderful is this country at the series “GO WITH US” and read about the advantages of travelling with group of people at “DON’T TRAVEL ALONE”
