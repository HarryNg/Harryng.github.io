Marriage in Finland



  1. Marriage is one of the most important milestones in people’s life. It doesn’t have influences on the present but also in the future, to the next generation and so on.
  2. This blog will help to find out some information about marriage in Finland - the happiest country in the world.

 

1. Marriage (meaning and definition)

  • Marriage is defined differently, and by different entities, based on cultural, religious, and personal factors.
  • A generally accepted and overarching definition of marriage is as follows: a formal and social and legal association between two individuals that aligns their lives legally, economically and affection. A contractual marriage agreement generally implies that a couple has legal obligations to each other for the rest of their life or until they decide to divorce.
  • Marriage also brings legitimacy to sexual relations within a marriage. Traditionally, marriage is often considered to have a key role in preserving morality and civilization.
  • Marriage is not allowed if the person is already married or has a registered partnership. Laws do not allow polygamy. Marriage is not allowed between close relatives either.

2. Finnish marriage

    1. According to Finnish law, everyone may get married in Finland if they are at least 18 years old. Marriages between two persons of the same sex are also allowed since March 2017.

    2. Marriage in Finland is always a voluntary decision and nobody should be forced into it. Unlike what happens in other cultures, parents are not allowed to pressure or force their child into marriage. In fact, forcing someone to marry is a crime in Finland.

    3. Marriage requirements and impediments:

  • Couples need to follow some steps to get married. First, an examination of the obstacles to marriage is conducted for all couples. This ensures that there are no legal or other obstacles mentioned in the Finnish Marriage Act.

 

  • You should submit an application to check your obstacles at the appropriate time before the date you intend to get married. The authority that issued these certificates is the Digital and Population Data Service.

 

  • If the Census Information System does not contain information about you or your spouse's marital status, you must obtain and submit a marital status certificate issued by the relevant authority of the host country.

 

  • Proof of marital status must be original and be valid for less than four months unless the certificate has another validity period. According to the Digital and Digital Data Services, a marital status certificate is not legalized if it is issued by the embassy or consulate of your naturalized country or Member State. EU.

 

  • Certifications can be accepted in Finnish, Swedish and English. If necessary, it must be translated into one of these languages. Translations by authorized Finnish translators do not need to be legalized, but translations made abroad must be legalized unless the translation is done by a qualified translator. perform its duties under the laws of an EU Member State.

 

  • The marriage problems test took at least seven days. But the authorities warn that if one of the couple's members is a foreigner, they should wait a few weeks for the process. The certificate is valid for four months from the date of issue. Obstacles can be checked from the Digital and Population Data Services Agency, formerly known as Maistraatti or the local registry offices.

CONCLUSION

Marriage is the most important event of a human’s life. It can bring many good or bad things in the future. That’s why each of us has to choose correctly and carefully. If you have interests in finding information about Finland, just follow our website.
