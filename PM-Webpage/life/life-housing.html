HOUSING IN FINLAND

 

If you have already planned for migrating to Finland, you will get in trouble with the next thing: housing.

There are many types of housing for you to choose from in Finland, but how to find the most suitable one that meets our criteria, such as budget, location, etc is truly confusing and stressful.

Reading this article, you will get the whole picture of all types of housing for foreigners to make the best decision for yourself.

 

1) Rental homes 

a. General information

 

It is estimated that about one-quarter of Finns live in rental accommodation. It is available in the flat of apartments, normal houses or also houses with terraces as well. Also, some foreigners can be given a rental home when working in this country by their company.

Home rentals must be done through companies, private persons, insurance companies, or the company that you are working for.

The price is not too high and very fit with the foreigners budget. Moreover, for people who want to experience more and don’t want to stay in a permanent space, a rental home is a good option that you must pay attention to. However, finding a flat that meets your requirements in the big busy cities is not easy and cheap as well. So you must take time for this exploring and quickly decide whether you rent or not because it can be sold out very quickly.

One special thing in Finland is that base on the Finnish law, the landlords are avoided from choosing the tenant through discrimination, religion, or nationality.

To get the rental home, you can meet some online advertisements on the website: https://www.vuokraovi.com/?locale=en 

 

b. Rental home options:

  • Private rental housing:

Private rental housing is renting the house from a private person (landlord), who owns the houses or flats.

You can rent the flat through the agency. If you want to find the flat easier and quicker, you can contact the agency for help. For that, you have to make a contract to pay the commission for their work. The agreement and commission must be in advance with both.

Besides, you can rent the flat by yourself. Just looking for the information on the website or newspaper, you can directly contact the landlord and make the meeting to check the flat. If you are interested, you have to complete the application form given by the landlord.

Generally, the process is not too difficult to rent private housing

  • Municipal rental housing

Municipal rental housing is the rent for the house that is provided by the municipalities. For that, it seems more affordable than the private ones, but much slower because you have to wait in a long list of that area rental.

To apply for state-subsidized rental housing, you need to be:

  • a Finnish citizenship
  • a registered right of residence;
  • a residence permit card;
  • a residence permit which is valid for at least 12 months; or
  • a student’s residence permit.

To apply for municipal rental housing normally, you just need to complete the application form. Some agencies offer the online form on their website, so you can easily fill in by yourself.

When choosing the tenants, they may focus on your: income, assets and requirements. For that, finding one in a short time is not possible.

  • Student housing

Student housing is very comfortable for students due to its lower price. It is provided by the Student housing foundation, Student union, Student Association and so on. 

You can apply for student housing as soon as you receive the study place because the process may take several weeks or months before you can move to live there. 

You can live in your rental house during your study time, but you have to make sure that the class in university is completed, if not, your lessor can individually stop the agreements.

Especially, if you are eligible for Finnish social security, you can contact for the housing allowance from Kella that will cover all your housing costs. 

Information about the Finnish social security https://www.infofinland.fi/en/living-in-finland/settling-in-finland/finnish-social-security

Information about housing allowance by Kella: https://www.infofinland.fi/en/living-in-finland/housing/housing-allowance 

 

 

2) Buying a home in Finland

 

a. General information

Buying a home is more advantageous than renting a home in a long-term aspect. 

You can find the home through a private person, real estate agencies or house constructors. All the information is published on the websites or newspaper. After choosing the most interesting one, you can make a meeting with them to see the real house or flat. If both meet the requirements, you will make an agreement and buy that home

b. Buying home options

There are two options for your purchase: real estate or housing share.

  • Real estate: 

When you buy real estate, it means that you buy a detached house and some more relevant plot.

To own this real estate, you have to check for eligibility and permission from the Finnish Ministry of Defence here

It will be easier to find an agency to support your process in English or other languages.

You have to pay for:

  • repayments and interest on your mortgage
  • real estate tax 
  • transfer tax (4% of the debt-free price of the home)
  • water rates and electricity charges
  • heating costs
  • waste collection fees
  • possible repairs.
  • House sharing

House sharing is the house in either blocks or flats that belongs to the housing companies or cooperatives.

You have to pay for:

  • repayments and interest on your mortgage
  • maintenance fee 
  • transfer tax (2% of the debt-free price of the home)
  • charge for financial costs (rahoitusvastike) if the housing company holds debt
  • water rates and electricity charges
  • possible repairs.

 

 

3) Right - of - occupancy home

 

Right - of - occupancy home is where you can live with the 15% payment of the whole price at first. After that, each month, you have to pay for the rest with a certain amount 

This type of home is common in the big areas, but the users have to queue a number of housing offices in the municipality. You can also queue in several municipalities as well.

If the provider doesn’t stick to the State, they can freely choose the occupants and in contrast, if the state supports the construction of the home, there are some criteria to find the suitable occupant.

 

 

4) Shared-ownership home?

 

A Shared-ownership home is a way of buying a house. At first, you just need to pay for a part of the house’s total price (normally 10-15%). After that, you can move to this house and live as a tenant and follow all the tenant’s laws. The payment for every month is not included in the total price of the house. During that time, you can buy more shares if you agree with the constructor and the living place. Living for a period of time (usually 5-12 years), you are able to buy the rest of the house to complete the buying process. Now, you are the owner of that house according to the housing company.

 

 

To sum up, each person has a different choice suitable for their budget and finance. Through this article, you will have a general knowledge of housing in Finland.
