TOP 10 DON’Ts IN FINLAND

 

Finland is very famous as a modern and civilized country. Many people here are very surprised at what people treat each other and how they communicate in society.

Finns very much respect the awareness and perception of one individual that they can evaluate that person. For that, many foreigners may get in trouble with the way to act in Finland at the first time.

This article will give you 10 things that you shouldn’t do in Finland to avoid bad impression when living there with the local

 

1) Interrupt the conversation

 

This is one of the rudest actions in Finns’ eyes. They hate being interrupted by others and consider it as impoliteness. In Finland, interruption is unacceptable although in many countries, we don’t mind that. 

For that, you should listen carefully and wait till the speakers stop. 

 

2) Being late

 

Finns are very punctual because to them, time is respectful and meaningful. They don’t like waiting for others. Therefore, in the meetings, they will start at the exact time. If you are later, you will receive some bad impressions from other colleagues as well as your manager.

To avoid this bad, you just need to well-arrange your timetable and willing to be in time (before the setting time 5-10 minutes)

 

3) Compare Finland with other countries

 

Finns are those who have strong national strong-respect. They are proud of all their wartime achievements and significant sporting merits. For that, they seem don’t like the comparison, especially Swedish and Russian. As the historic events, Swedish and Russian are used to invade their country, so they are not completely have a good sight of these two countries. Also, in sports, you should try to avoid commenting on their hockey team and comparing it with Swedish team. It seems very rude.

All you need to do is try to control your saying and make as many true compliments for them as you can. 

 

4) Break the social distance?

 

Finns are very famous for their social distance. The privacy in the public is always respected. In some public areas, such as: bus station, supermarkets, they try to keep the distance to each other of 4,7 inches, which seems very far to Asian. Moreover, don’t try to access them if you are not related to them. But asking for the direction is acceptable.

You should notice this point because it will take place in every moment in your daily life.

 

5) Impolite with women

 

Women are treated equally to men in either business or home. Finns give a respectful view to the women and are proud of many women heroes in their countries. Sometimes, women may be treated much better than men like in a couple, women are always greeted first. For that reason, any impoliteness or gender racism is considered as rude and disregarded. 

Be always polite and kind to women in Finland, you will make good impressions. 

 

6) Eating before the host

 

Whenever you are invited to the private party, it is necessary to notice the rituals sequences to behave correctly. You should not eat until all food is served as well as drink until the wishing from the host. It is more appreciated if you start your meals after the host.

Be humble and notice anything in the party will give you the right behaviours.

 

7) Show strong emotion

 

You shouldn’t show your strong emotion, because Finns view this as unprofessional. Moreover, strong emotion may harm your relationship and your business. 

Try to be calm and cooperative to decide the most reasonable key.

 

8) Over-tip

 

Paying tips is so familiar in many countries that paying an extra amount to the server. But in Finland, not all services receive tips from you. Especially when you over-tipping in some places, the server will not know how to react and believe that you are making a mistake. 

In some cases that you are in doubt about tipping, don’t hesitate to simply ask them whether a tip is accepted here.

 

9) Be bragging

 

Finns are very modest and frank in spite of their great achievement. For that, they also don’t like people who are bragging too much. They especially have a low tolerance of self-important braggarts. Therefore, they highly evaluate the modesty from others and consider it as one of the essential characteristics in daily life and business as well

While interacting with Finns, try to avoid bragging yourself too much that they will have a bad impression with you instead of the good one you want to gain.

 

10) Small talk

 

Finns don’t like small talk because it makes the story go awkward. If you want to say anything, just go straight to the issues, and if you don’t know what to say, silence is much appreciated. Your talk must be the elevator, not to mark something to the speaker.

To avoid this, you need to carefully think of the conversation before hold that to the speakers because it must be valued to both of you.

 

These are 10 common things that you should notice when living in Finland to avoid bad impression to your locals, friend or partners . And if you are interested in some tips to communicate in FInland, try accessing “TIPS FOR FINLAND LIVING” or “DAILY COMMUNICATION”
